the problem

five tools, no single source of truth

parabola's design system lived in figma. the canonical rendered components lived in storybook. spec handoff happened in zeplin. visual regression ran through chromatic. and the tokens — the actual colours, spacings, and radii — were copy-pasted into a notion wiki maintained by a single design-ops manager who held the whole mapping in her head.

by q3 2023 the team was spending roughly 40% of design-to-ship time on the sync step itself. new hires took six to eight weeks to become productive. the notion wiki had 320 pages and three flagged as "do not trust".

  • figma components and storybook stories drifted weekly; no automated reconciliation.
  • tokens existed in four places, with no authoritative copy.
  • a single person held institutional memory of the mapping.
  • new engineers hit the "which one is right?" blocker inside their first sprint.
the evaluation

what we looked at, and why untitled won

parabola evaluated four options over twelve weeks: building the sync pipeline in-house, adopting a larger all-in-one vendor, staying put with improved process, and moving to untitled.

the deciding factor was not features. every vendor had tokens, every vendor had storybook-style preview. the deciding factor was the token diff surface — the ability to see what a change in figma did to the shipped css before the pr merged.

"the moment we saw a token change in figma open a pr with the exact rendered css diff attached — that was the sale. we'd built something like it in-house twice and given up both times."
MH
Mira Hakimi head of design, parabola
the rollout

pilot to full adoption in eight weeks

the pilot ran with a single product team (12 people) for four weeks. no migration — they opened untitled alongside their existing stack and used both.

by week 6 the pilot team was opening prs with automatic token diffs. by week 8 the rest of the design org had requested access. full migration completed by end of q1.

  • week 1: connect figma library, map to the react workspace.
  • weeks 2-4: run dual-write in the pilot team.
  • weeks 5-6: pilot produces first three prs with native diffs.
  • weeks 7-8: broader design org opts in; design-ops manager migrates the notion wiki in two days.
the architecture

what replaced what

by end of q1, five tools had been consolidated into untitled plus a trimmed chromatic installation for visual regression only. storybook retired. zeplin cancelled. the notion wiki archived in full after its content was ported into the untitled workspace.

  • figma tokens stayed in figma — untitled syncs them out.
  • component spec + visual state lives in untitled, not a second render target.
  • chromatic retained for snapshot coverage of legacy product areas.
  • notion wiki archived; onboarding docs now live next to the components.
"retiring storybook was the one people worried about. we ran both for six weeks. after that, the team volunteered to stop."
RS
Reni Solberg staff engineer, parabola
the outcome

one year in

twelve months after full adoption: new-hire time-to-productive-pr dropped from six weeks to under ten days. design-to-code handoff went from an average of 11 working days to 2.8. the notion wiki that used to hold institutional memory is archived and no-one has opened it since august.

the finance team's 2024 efficiency audit credited the consolidation with $1.2m of recovered engineering time — roughly 16 fte-weeks across the year, at a blended rate that parabola publishes in its diversity report.

what we'd change

not a puff piece

the rollout wasn't frictionless. three things mira would do differently:

  • start the wiki port earlier — the institutional-memory chunk took a full week to capture cleanly.
  • set a hard retirement date for storybook at the start, not the end.
  • bring finance and compliance into the soc-2 review in week 2, not week 12.
