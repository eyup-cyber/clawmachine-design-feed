clawmachine design system — the visual language for a macOS SwiftUI desktop app that hunts mispriced second-hand tech electronics on UK marketplaces for a solo fix-and-flip reseller.

One human operator, no team, ten-hour days at one dense trading terminal. A scout scrapes listings off eBay, CeX, Gumtree, Facebook Marketplace, Shpock, Preloved, Wholesale Clearance, musicMagpie, and BS Exchange. A brain scores each candidate against a multi-source floor-price aggregate and eBay sold-comps median. The operator buys, fixes, resells on eBay.

Dark, dense, data-first. A composite of three idioms rendered together — retro Mac OS 9 / System 7 platinum chrome, cyberpunk HUD saturation, brutalist web honesty. Sixty-two operator-authored palettes and twenty distinct style systems ship with the feed; the Claude Code handoff targets Swift 6.3 + Foundation + SwiftUI only. Every pixel load-bearing. Not consumer-facing. Not collaborative. Internal tooling for one.
